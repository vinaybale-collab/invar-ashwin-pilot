Investor summary

What this company faces, what drives it, and where it concentrates. Charts and line-by-line derivation follow for auditors.

GODAWARI POWER AND ISPAT LIMITED faces an estimated 582.8 Cr of gross nature-related value at risk over a 5Y disorderly transition scenario. That is 11.6% of annual revenue (on a 5,042 Cr base). On a sector EBITDA proxy, the same shock equates to roughly 41.3% of operating profit at risk — a useful IC screening ratio, not audited EBITDA. The plausible range spans 527.8–642.5 Cr, so treat the headline as central, not precise.

The dominant channel is flood protection from natural buffers, accounting for about 33% of this company's nature exposure. Where natural flood buffers weaken, business interruption and asset damage risk rises at river-adjacent and low-lying facilities. A secondary driver is air quality and particulate exposure (24%). Little disclosed mitigation is applied in the headline number, so this reads as gross downside exposure.

Exposure is geolocated across 5 facilities in India. The largest single site — GODAWARI POWER AND ISPAT LIMITED::EST_2 — carries about 43% of facility-level NVaR (295.6 Cr). The number moves if local water stress worsens, if sector dependency assumptions shift, or if additional mitigation is verified — full mechanics are in the derivation table below.

We classify input evidence as Tier A (92/100) — Tier A means the strongest input coverage in this pilot (BRSR revenue, geocoded facilities, calibrated dependencies).
