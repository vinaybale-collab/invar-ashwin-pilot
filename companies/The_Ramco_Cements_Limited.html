Investor summary · nature-related revenue exposure

The Ramco Cements Limited has an estimated gross nature value at risk (NVaR) of 1,748 Cr (Monte Carlo median) under a DISORDERLY transition over 5Y. That is equivalent to 12.6% of annual revenue (13,826 Cr). Using a sector EBITDA margin proxy, this maps to roughly 105.3% of proxy EBITDA at risk (screening headline — not audited EBITDA). The uncertainty band runs from 1,635 Cr (5th percentile) to 1,866 Cr (95th percentile).

Nature-related revenue exposure is driven primarily by flood protection from natural buffers (26% of the exposure ratio), water availability and groundwater stress (22% of the exposure ratio), air quality and particulate exposure (19% of the exposure ratio), and climate regulation and temperature extremes (16% of the exposure ratio). Each channel combines sector dependency on the ecosystem service (India-calibrated ENCORE/MoSPI matrix), optional BRSR-reported mitigation, and location-specific spatial stress at geocoded facilities.

Flood protection from natural buffers: dependency D=0.482, no mitigation applied in headline (M≈0), spatial stress Δσ=0.080 → contribution 0.0388 to the exposure ratio. Water availability and groundwater stress: dependency D=0.580, mitigation M=0.69 (BRSR-adjusted), spatial stress Δσ=0.186 → contribution 0.0331 to the exposure ratio. Air quality and particulate exposure: dependency D=0.469, no mitigation applied in headline (M≈0), spatial stress Δσ=0.060 → contribution 0.0283 to the exposure ratio.

Exposure is mapped across 10 geocoded facilities using India-specific spatial layers (CGWB district groundwater, NII GIS water stress, WRI Aqueduct baseline, and GLOBIO biodiversity where applicable). Facility-level splits appear in the charts below and in the detailed tables.
