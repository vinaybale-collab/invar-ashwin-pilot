Manappuram Finance Limited has an estimated gross nature value at risk (NVaR) of 195.8 Cr (Monte Carlo median) under a DISORDERLY transition over 5Y. That is equivalent to 2.8% of annual revenue (6,914 Cr). The uncertainty band runs from 182.4 Cr (5th percentile) to 210.0 Cr (95th percentile).

Nature-related revenue exposure is driven primarily by water availability and groundwater stress (46% of the exposure ratio), climate regulation and temperature extremes (27% of the exposure ratio), flood protection from natural buffers (16% of the exposure ratio), and soil health and land productivity (10% of the exposure ratio). Each channel combines sector dependency on the ecosystem service (India-calibrated ENCORE/MoSPI matrix), optional BRSR-reported mitigation, and location-specific spatial stress at geocoded facilities.

Climate regulation and temperature extremes: dependency D=0.250, no mitigation applied in headline (M≈0), spatial stress Δσ=0.054 → contribution 0.0134 to the exposure ratio. Flood protection from natural buffers: dependency D=0.100, no mitigation applied in headline (M≈0), spatial stress Δσ=0.080 → contribution 0.0080 to the exposure ratio.
