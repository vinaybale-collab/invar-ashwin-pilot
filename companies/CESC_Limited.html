CESC Limited has an estimated gross nature value at risk (NVaR) of 738.1 Cr (Monte Carlo median) under a DISORDERLY transition over 5Y. That is equivalent to 7.6% of annual revenue (9,765 Cr). Using a sector EBITDA margin proxy, this maps to roughly 54.0% of proxy EBITDA at risk (screening headline — not audited EBITDA). The uncertainty band runs from 688.8 Cr (5th percentile) to 789.0 Cr (95th percentile).

Nature-related revenue exposure is driven primarily by flood protection from natural buffers (35% of the exposure ratio), soil health and land productivity (22% of the exposure ratio), climate regulation and temperature extremes (22% of the exposure ratio), and air quality and particulate exposure (16% of the exposure ratio). Each channel combines sector dependency on the ecosystem service (India-calibrated ENCORE/MoSPI matrix), optional BRSR-reported mitigation, and location-specific spatial stress at geocoded facilities.
